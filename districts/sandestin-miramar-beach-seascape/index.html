District 08

Sandestin, Miramar Beach & Seascape Stormwater District

Sandestin, Miramar Beach, and Seascape form 30A's western gateway: resort campuses, condos, neighborhoods, beach roads, and bay-connected drainage all sharing low coastal ground. The practical question is simple: where does stormwater go after a hard rain, how is it treated before it reaches the Gulf or Choctawhatchee Bay, and which public fixes can reduce flooding without shifting the problem downstream?

Local priorities

What this district page tracks.

These topics anchor Sandestin, Miramar Beach, and Seascape show notes, video descriptions, podcast metadata, and future embeds.

  • Heavy rain can overwhelm low-lying beach roads, parking areas, and access points, creating standing water where residents, guests, workers, and emergency vehicles all need reliable movement.
  • Large roofs, paved lots, service drives, and resort-scale circulation areas move runoff quickly; treatment and storage matter before that water reaches Gulf-facing drains or bay-connected systems.
  • Public roads and private stormwater systems often sit side by side in this district, so maintenance responsibility, inspection, and project sequencing need to be clear.
  • Scenic Gulf Drive, US 98 connections, and beach access corridors concentrate traffic and runoff in the same places; drainage fixes should protect mobility as well as water quality.
  • Sandestin, Miramar Beach, and Seascape drain toward both Gulf and bay waters, so projects should be basin-specific, measured, and designed to keep polluted runoff out of receiving waters.
