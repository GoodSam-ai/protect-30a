Grayton Beach & Blue Mountain Beach Stormwater District

Grayton Beach and Blue Mountain Beach sit between some of South Walton's most loved neighborhoods, beach accesses, and rare coastal dune lakes. A dedicated stormwater district would help keep runoff cleaner and slower before it reaches Western Lake, Alligator Lake, Big Redfish Lake, Little Redfish Lake, Draper Lake, and the Gulf.

Local priorities

What this district page tracks.

These topics anchor Grayton Beach and Blue Mountain Beach show notes, video descriptions, podcast metadata, and future embeds.

  • Heavy rain can move quickly from rooftops, driveways, parking areas, and 30A into low streets, lake basins, and beach outfalls.
  • Western Lake, Alligator Lake, Big Redfish Lake, Little Redfish Lake, and Draper Lake need cleaner upstream runoff to protect water quality and habitat.
  • Older drainage patterns were not built for today's traffic, redevelopment, impervious surface, and storm intensity.
  • Public beach access areas, bike-path corridors, and neighborhood roads need drainage that works without pushing untreated water into the lakes.
  • Local funding should prioritize visible, practical fixes inside the district: swales, inlets, culverts, treatment areas, and maintenance.
