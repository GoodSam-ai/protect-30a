Santa Rosa Beach, Gulf Place & Dune Allen Stormwater District

On the west end of 30A, Santa Rosa Beach, Gulf Place, and Dune Allen sit where neighborhood streets, beach access points, restaurants, trails, and rare dune lakes all share the same drainage system. Cleaner, slower stormwater here protects Oyster, Allen, Stallworth, and nearby Topsail watersheds before runoff reaches the Gulf.

Local priorities

What this district page tracks.

These topics anchor Santa Rosa Beach, Gulf Place, and Dune Allen show notes, video descriptions, podcast metadata, and future embeds.

  • Heavy rain moves quickly off 30A pavement, parking areas, rooftops, and compacted lots toward low streets, beach accesses, and lake basins.
  • Oyster Lake, Allen Lake, and Stallworth Lake have little room for untreated runoff because each sits close to roads, homes, commercial activity, and the Gulf.
  • The Gulf Place and Ed Walline area concentrates daily traffic, parking, pedestrians, and hard surfaces in a small coastal footprint.
  • Drainage work needs to be planned by watershed, not just by road segment, so fixes upstream do not push water problems downstream.
  • Existing and proposed projects, including Allen Loop drainage work, need a durable local funding path for construction, treatment, and long-term maintenance.
